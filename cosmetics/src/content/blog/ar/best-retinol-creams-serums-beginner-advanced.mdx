## How to Build Your Retinol Ladder

**Months 1-3:** Start with a beginner product, every other night. Buffer with moisturizer if needed.

**Months 4-6:** Increase to nightly use. If no irritation after one month of nightly use, you're ready for intermediate.

**Months 7-12:** Move to an intermediate product. Start every other night again, then increase.

**Month 12+:** If your skin tolerates intermediate nightly without issues, consider moving to retinaldehyde.

**Critical rules:** Always use SPF 30+ during the day when using any retinoid. Never combine retinol with AHAs/BHAs in the same routine step. If you experience persistent peeling, redness, or stinging, drop back one tier. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between retinol and retinal?

Retinol must convert twice in the skin (retinol > retinaldehyde > retinoic acid) to become active. Retinal (retinaldehyde) only needs one conversion, making it faster-acting and up to 11x more potent at equivalent concentrations. Despite being stronger, retinal often causes less irritation because of the shorter conversion pathway.

### Can you use retinol in your 20s?

Yes, but start low. Preventive retinol use in your mid-to-late 20s can delay the earliest signs of aging. Stick to beginner-tier products (0.1% or lower) and focus on consistency over potency.

### How long until retinol shows results?

Expect 8-12 weeks for visible improvements in fine lines, texture, and tone at beginner concentrations. Higher concentrations and retinaldehyde products can show results in 4-6 weeks. The full collagen-rebuilding benefit takes 6-12 months of consistent use.
