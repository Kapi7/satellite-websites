We wore each sunscreen daily for a month — outdoors, under makeup, through humid weather and dry office air — to figure out which one actually deserves the top spot. The answer depends entirely on your skin type and what finish you want.

## Head-to-Head by Category

### UV Protection

Both are SPF50+ PA++++. Both tested reliably in our UV camera checks. In terms of raw protection, we couldn't find a meaningful difference. The BoJ hybrid formula may offer slightly broader spectrum coverage from the physical filter component, but in daily practical use, application consistency matters more than filter type.

**Winner.** Tie.

### Finish and Appearance

This is the defining difference. BoJ gives you glow. Skin1004 gives you invisibility.

If you want your skin to look dewy, lit, and healthy — like you slept 10 hours and drank a gallon of water — Beauty of Joseon delivers that in a single step. If you want your sunscreen to disappear completely and let your skin look normal (or set the stage for a matte makeup look), Skin1004 is the answer.

**Winner.** Subjective. BoJ for glow-seekers. Skin1004 for natural-matte lovers.

### Comfort Through the Day

**Skin1004** felt more comfortable across all skin types. It never felt heavy, never got shiny on oily testers, and didn't dry out our dry-skin tester's face. It just sat there, doing its job, unfelt.

**BoJ** was comfortable on dry and normal skin but became noticeably shiny on our oily-skin testers by the 4-hour mark. In humid conditions, the dewiness intensified past the point of looking intentional.

**Winner.** Skin1004.

### Skincare Benefits

**BoJ** has the edge here. The rice bran extract genuinely brightens over time (our testers noticed improved tone after three weeks), and the probiotic ferment supports the skin barrier. You're getting a treatment product and a sunscreen in one.

**Skin1004** provides soothing benefits from centella, which is meaningful for acne-prone and sensitive skin but less dramatic than the brightening BoJ delivers.

**Winner.** BoJ for brightening. Skin1004 for calming.

### Value

Nearly identical pricing at $10–$17 for 50ml from either brand. Both are extraordinary value compared to Western sunscreens in the same performance tier. We covered both extensively in our [no-white-cast sunscreen roundup](/best-korean-sunscreens-oily-skin-no-white-cast/).

**Winner.** Tie.

### For Deeper Skin Tones

Both sunscreens performed well with no white cast across all skin tones tested, from fair to deep. BoJ's dewy finish can look particularly beautiful on medium and deeper skin tones, adding luminosity without any gray or ashy undertone. Skin1004 is truly invisible on everyone. If white cast has been a concern with previous sunscreens, both are safe choices.

**Winner.** Both excellent. Slight edge to Skin1004 for absolute invisibility.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do these sunscreens work for sensitive skin?

Both are generally well-tolerated by sensitive skin. Skin1004 has a slight edge because centella is specifically anti-inflammatory. If your skin reacts to chemical filters, note that BoJ uses a hybrid formula (chemical + physical) which some sensitive skin types tolerate better. Patch test either way. For more sensitive-skin SPF options, see our [Korean sunscreens for acne-prone skin](/korean-sunscreens-acne-prone-skin/) guide.

### Can I use these under cushion foundations?

Yes. Both work beautifully under [cushion foundations](/best-korean-cushion-foundations-skin-type/). BoJ adds luminosity that makes dewy cushions look even better. Skin1004 creates a smooth, non-interfering base that lets matte and satin cushions perform as intended.

### How much should I apply?

Two finger-lengths (index and middle finger, from base to tip) for face and neck. Both products spread well enough that this amount covers adequately without looking heavy. Underapplication is the most common sunscreen mistake — more product matters more than which product you choose.

### Do I need to double-cleanse to remove these?

BoJ's hybrid formula (containing physical filters) benefits from double-cleansing. An oil cleanser or [cleansing balm](/best-korean-cleansing-balms-oils-guide/) will remove it more thoroughly than a water-based cleanser alone. Skin1004's fully chemical formula removes more easily with a standard gentle cleanser, though double-cleansing is still a good practice.

### Are these reef-safe?

Both avoid oxybenzone and octinoxate, the two filters most commonly flagged for coral reef damage. However, "reef-safe" has no regulated definition. If reef safety is a priority, check the full ingredient list against your preferred standard.
