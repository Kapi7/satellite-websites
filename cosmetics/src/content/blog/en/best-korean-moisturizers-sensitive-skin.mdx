## What Sensitive Skin Actually Needs

**Barrier repair.** Sensitive skin almost always has a compromised moisture barrier. The barrier is the outer layer of skin that keeps moisture in and irritants out. When it's damaged, everything stings, skin loses water faster, and inflammation becomes chronic.

**Ceramides.** These are the lipids (fats) that make up 50% of your skin barrier. Replenishing ceramides is the most direct way to repair and strengthen the barrier.

**Anti-inflammatory ingredients.** Centella asiatica, madecassoside, panthenol (vitamin B5), and allantoin calm existing irritation while the barrier heals.

**What to avoid.** Fragrance, alcohol denat, essential oils (even "natural" ones like tea tree or lavender), high concentrations of active acids, and products with 30+ ingredients.

## How to Moisturize Sensitive Skin

1. **Apply to damp skin.** Right after toner, while your skin is still slightly wet. This locks in more hydration and reduces the chance of the moisturizer "pulling" on dry skin.
2. **Use gentle pressure.** Press the moisturizer into your skin rather than rubbing. Friction is an irritant for sensitive skin.
3. **Don't skip moisturizer in summer.** Even oily-sensitive skin needs moisture. Switch to a lighter gel-cream instead of dropping the step entirely.
4. **Patch test everything.** Apply a new product to a small area behind your ear or on your inner forearm for 48 hours before using it on your face.

### Are Korean moisturizers good for sensitive skin?

Yes. Korean formulators have built entire product lines around sensitive skin — PURITO's Dermide range, ETUDE's SoonJung line, and Dr.Jart+'s Ceramidin series are all designed around barrier repair, fragrance-free formulations, and calming ingredients like centella, panthenol, and madecassoside. ### What ingredients should sensitive skin avoid in moisturizers?

Avoid fragrance (listed as "parfum" or "fragrance"), alcohol denat, essential oils (including tea tree, lavender, and citrus oils), and high concentrations of active acids like AHA or BHA. Also skip products with 30+ ingredients — longer lists mean more opportunities for a reaction. If you're not sure whether an ingredient is safe, cross-check it against our [skincare ingredient compatibility guide](/skincare-ingredient-compatibility-guide/).

### Is Korean moisturizer better than Western moisturizer for sensitive skin?

For sensitive skin specifically, yes — in most cases. Korean moisturizers tend to use lighter textures, simpler ingredient lists, and calming botanical extracts (centella, heartleaf, mugwort) that are harder to find in Western formulations. They also avoid fragrance more consistently in their "sensitive" ranges. The exceptions are clinical Western brands like CeraVe and La Roche-Posay, which are also excellent.

### Can sensitive skin use a ceramide moisturizer daily?

Yes, and it's actively recommended. Ceramides are the lipids that make up roughly 50% of your skin barrier. Replenishing them daily is the most direct way to repair and maintain a healthy barrier. Sensitive skin almost always has a compromised barrier, which is why ceramide-based moisturizers like PURITO Dermide or Dr.Jart+ Ceramidin are ideal as daily use.

### Do Korean moisturizers for sensitive skin work for rosacea?

Many do. The ingredients that help sensitive skin — ceramides, panthenol, centella, madecassoside — are also the ones most often recommended for rosacea. The Anua Heartleaf 70% Soothing Cream and SKIN1004 Madagascar Centella Soothing Cream are particularly popular with rosacea-prone users because of their high concentrations of anti-inflammatory botanicals. ### How long does it take for a Korean moisturizer to calm sensitive skin?

Initial calming (reduced stinging, less redness) usually shows within 1–2 weeks of consistent use. Full barrier repair takes 4–6 weeks, which is roughly one skin-cell turnover cycle. If your skin is still reacting after 2 weeks of use with a fragrance-free, gentle product, the problem is likely another product in your routine — not the moisturizer.

### Are these Korean moisturizers safe during pregnancy?

Most on this list are pregnancy-safe. Ceramides, panthenol, squalane, centella, heartleaf, and hyaluronic acid are all considered safe during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Always check the full ingredient list against your doctor's guidance, particularly if the product contains retinol derivatives, salicylic acid, or any active acids. The products on this list are free of those ingredients.
