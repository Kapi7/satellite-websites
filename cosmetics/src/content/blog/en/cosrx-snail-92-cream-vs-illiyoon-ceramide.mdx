Two moisturizers. Two completely different philosophies. The COSRX Advanced Snail 92 All-in-One Cream banks on snail mucin's multi-tasking repair, while the ILLIYOON Ceramide Ato Concentrate Cream bets everything on barrier-first ceramide technology. Both have earned cult status in K-beauty circles, and both regularly appear in "holy grail moisturizer" threads across every skincare community online.

We tested each for six weeks on five team members with skin types ranging from oily-dehydrated to severely dry and eczema-prone. Here's what we found.

## Head-to-Head by Category

### Hydration

**COSRX** delivers immediate, visible plumpness. Your skin looks bouncy and hydrated within minutes of application. The snail mucin attracts water and creates a light film that holds moisture in.

**ILLIYOON** takes longer to show visual results, but the hydration runs deeper and lasts longer. Ceramides don't just sit on top — they integrate into your skin's own lipid structure and strengthen its ability to retain moisture independently.

**Winner.** ILLIYOON for lasting hydration. COSRX for instant visible plumpness.

### Barrier Repair

Not close. ILLIYOON is specifically engineered for barrier repair. The ceramide-cholesterol complex mirrors the skin's natural lipid composition. If your barrier is compromised — from over-exfoliation, retinol use, harsh weather, or conditions like eczema — ILLIYOON is the clinical-grade answer.

COSRX's snail mucin does support repair, but it works more on surface-level healing (post-acne marks, minor irritation) than deep barrier restoration.

**Winner.** ILLIYOON.

### Texture and Wearability

COSRX wins for daytime use. Its lightweight gel-cream sits invisibly under [sunscreen](/best-korean-sunscreens-oily-skin-no-white-cast/) and makeup. ILLIYOON is heavier and can feel too rich for morning routines, especially on oily or combination skin.

For nighttime, ILLIYOON's richness becomes an asset. It acts like a sleeping pack, sealing everything in overnight.

**Winner.** COSRX for day. ILLIYOON for night.

### Value

ILLIYOON gives you double the product for a similar price. At 200ml versus 100g, the per-ml cost of ILLIYOON is significantly lower. Both are affordable, but ILLIYOON stretches further.

**Winner.** ILLIYOON.

### Versatility

COSRX works on nearly every skin type and slots into any [routine](/korean-skincare-routine-complete-guide/) without friction. It hydrates, repairs, and layers easily. ILLIYOON is specialized — incredible for dry and damaged skin, but potentially too heavy for oily types.

**Winner.** COSRX.

## Who Should Buy Which

**Choose COSRX Snail 92 if:**
- You have oily, combination, or normal skin
- You want a lightweight daily moisturizer that works under makeup and SPF
- You're looking for mild repair benefits (acne scars, redness)
- You prefer a dewy, bouncy finish
- You want something that fits any routine without adjustment

**Choose ILLIYOON Ceramide Cream if:**
- You have dry, very dry, or eczema-prone skin
- Your skin barrier is damaged or chronically compromised
- You need deep, long-lasting hydration that holds for 12+ hours
- You're recovering from retinol overuse, over-exfoliation, or harsh treatments
- You want a nighttime moisturizer that doubles as a sleeping pack

**Or use both.** Several of our testers settled on COSRX for mornings (lightweight, layers well) and ILLIYOON at night (deep repair, lasting moisture). The two creams complement each other perfectly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use COSRX Snail 92 Cream if I have dry skin?

Yes, but you may need to layer it over a hydrating toner or essence for enough moisture. On its own, it's better suited to oily and normal skin. Dry skin types should pair it with a hydrating step like the [COSRX Snail 96 Essence](https://mirai-skin.com/products/cosrx-advanced-snail-96-mucin-power-essence-100ml).

### Is ILLIYOON Ceramide Cream safe for acne-prone skin?

Yes. Despite its thick texture, the ILLIYOON Ceramide Cream is non-comedogenic and fragrance-free. Many acne-prone users with dry skin use it without breakouts. If you're oily and acne-prone, use a smaller amount or reserve it for nighttime only.

### Which cream is better for sensitive skin?

Both are fragrance-free and gentle, but ILLIYOON edges ahead for sensitive skin specifically because ceramides actively calm and restore the barrier. If your sensitivity comes from a weakened barrier (most does), ILLIYOON addresses the root cause.

### Can I layer these with retinol?

Absolutely. Both work well over retinol treatments. ILLIYOON is especially good as a buffer or follow-up to retinol because its ceramides help counteract the barrier disruption retinol can cause. See our [retinol beginner's guide](/retinol-for-beginners-start-here/) for layering tips.

### Do either of these have a white cast?

No. Neither product has any white cast. Both are transparent on the skin regardless of skin tone.
