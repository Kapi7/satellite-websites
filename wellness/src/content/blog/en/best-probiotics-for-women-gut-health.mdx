## What CFU Count Actually Means

CFU (colony forming units) measures how many live bacteria are in a dose. But here's what the research says:

- **Most clinical studies use 1–50 billion CFU.** Higher isn't inherently better.
- **Strain matters more than count.** 10 billion CFU of a clinically studied strain outperforms 100 billion of a random one.
- **Survivability matters.** If the bacteria die in stomach acid before reaching your intestines, CFU count is irrelevant. Look for enteric coating, delayed-release capsules, or acid-resistant strains.

**The sweet spot for most women: 10–50 billion CFU from clinically studied strains with a verified delivery mechanism.**

## How to Get the Most from Your Probiotic

1. **Take consistently** — probiotics need daily use for 4–8 weeks to establish measurable changes in gut flora
2. **Feed the bacteria** — prebiotics (fiber, fermented foods) are fuel. Without them, supplemented strains struggle to colonize
3. **Time it right** — take with food or just before a meal. Food buffers stomach acid and improves bacterial survival
4. **Store properly** — check the label. Some need refrigeration; shelf-stable formulas use specific encapsulation tech
5. **Don't double up blindly** — more products at once doesn't mean better results. Pick one well-formulated option
