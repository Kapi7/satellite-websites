## Cordless Leaf Blower Buying Guide

Choosing the right cordless leaf blower involves more than just picking the cheapest option. Here's what to consider:

### CFM Matters More Than MPH

When evaluating a leaf blower's power, **Cubic Feet per Minute (CFM)** is generally a more important metric than Miles Per Hour (MPH).
*   **CFM** indicates the *volume* of air the blower moves. High CFM means you can move more leaves at once, making it more efficient for large piles and open areas.
*   **MPH** indicates the *speed* of the air. High MPH is great for dislodging stubborn, wet, or heavy debris, or for precision work in tight spaces.
*   **The Sweet Spot:** Look for a good balance. A blower with high CFM will move more material, while sufficient MPH ensures it has the force to break free stuck debris. For most residential tasks, aim for 400+ CFM.

### Battery Commitment: Pick Your Platform Wisely

One of the biggest advantages (and considerations) of cordless tools is the battery platform.
*   **Ecosystem Advantage:** Once you buy into a brand's battery system (e.g., Ryobi 40V, DeWalt 20V MAX, EGO 56V), you can use the same batteries across multiple tools (lawnmowers, string trimmers, drills, etc.). This saves you money in the long run as you only need to buy bare tools.
*   **Cost Savings:** If you already own tools from a specific brand, prioritize their leaf blower. You can often buy the "bare tool" (without battery and charger) for significantly less, staying well within your budget.
*   **Future Proofing:** Consider which brand offers the range of tools you might need in the future.

### Brushless Motors: The Efficiency Edge

Many modern cordless leaf blowers feature brushless motors, and for good reason:
*   **Efficiency:** Brushless motors are more efficient than their brushed counterparts, meaning they convert more battery power into actual work. This translates to longer runtimes per charge.
*   **Power:** They often deliver more torque and power for their size, giving you better blowing performance.
*   **Durability:** Without physical brushes that wear out, brushless motors tend to have a longer lifespan and require less maintenance.
*   **Cooler Operation:** They run cooler, reducing wear and tear on components.

While brushed motors can still be found in budget models, a brushless motor is a strong indicator of a higher-quality, more performant cordless blower.

## Who Shouldn't Buy a Cordless Leaf Blower?

While cordless leaf blowers have come a long way, they're not for everyone. If you own a property **larger than 0.5 acre**, especially one with a significant number of trees, a cordless blower might leave you frustrated.

*   **Limited Runtime:** Even with the best batteries, cordless blowers have finite runtimes, and recharging can take hours. For extensive yard work, you'll likely need multiple expensive batteries or face significant downtime.
*   **Power for Large Scale:** While powerful, cordless models typically can't match the sustained, brute force of a high-end gas blower, especially for very dense, wet, or widespread leaf coverage.

For properties over half an acre, particularly those with heavy leaf fall, a **gas-powered leaf blower (or even a corded electric if you have ample outlets and don't mind the cord)** remains the more practical and efficient choice. You'll get more power, longer continuous runtimes (just refuel and go), and often a lower upfront cost for comparable power.

## FAQ: Cordless Leaf Blowers

### Q1: What's the main difference between CFM and MPH?
A1: CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute) measures the volume of air moved, ideal for sweeping large piles. MPH (Miles Per Hour) measures the speed of the air, good for dislodging stuck-on debris. You want a good balance of both.

### Q2: How long do cordless leaf blower batteries typically last?
A2: Runtime varies greatly by battery size (Ah) and power setting. On high, expect 15-25 minutes for a standard battery (2.0-2.5Ah). On lower settings, you might get 40-60 minutes. Larger batteries (4.0-5.0Ah) extend these times significantly.

### Q3: Can I use a cordless leaf blower for snow?
A3: For light, fluffy snow (1-2 inches), yes, a powerful leaf blower can be quite effective. It's not suitable for heavy, wet, or compacted snow, which requires a snow blower.

### Q4: Are cordless blowers powerful enough for wet leaves?
A4: Many modern cordless blowers, especially those with higher CFM and MPH ratings, can handle damp leaves. For very wet, matted leaves, you'll need a powerful model (like our Toro pick) and may require more passes than with a gas blower.

### Q5: What's the best way to maintain a cordless leaf blower?
A5: Keep the air intake clear of debris, store batteries in a cool, dry place, and keep them charged (but not necessarily at 100% for long-term storage). Clean the nozzle and housing as needed. No engine maintenance required!

### Q6: Is a higher voltage always better for cordless blowers?
A6: Not necessarily. While higher voltage (e.g., 60V vs. 20V) *can* indicate more power potential, the actual performance (CFM/MPH) and efficiency (brushless motor) are more crucial. A well-engineered 40V blower can outperform a poorly designed 60V one.

### Q7: What is a brushless motor and why does it matter?
A7: A brushless motor uses magnets and electronics instead of carbon brushes to generate power. This makes them more efficient, more powerful, runs cooler, and lasts longer than brushed motors, providing better overall performance and battery life.

### Q8: Should I get a handheld or backpack leaf blower?
A8: All our picks are handheld. Handheld blowers are lighter, more maneuverable, and ideal for most residential yards up to 0.5 acre. Backpack blowers are heavier and more powerful, designed for larger properties or extended use, but typically exceed the $200 budget.
